
A9 Learned No-reference Frame-level Selector:
To further test whether the small oracle headroom can be exploited by a stronger adaptive strategy, we trained a ridge-regression selector on calibration frames. The selector predicts candidate-level PSNR gains over FUGR-C using only no-reference features from BasicVSR/FUGR outputs. On the held-out test split, the best deployable policy remains FUGR-C. Aggressive learned selection slightly degrades PSNR and TDE, while conservative margins simply fall back to FUGR-C. The per-frame oracle still gives only about +0.0012 dB PSNR and slightly worsens TDE. Therefore, even supervised calibration is insufficient to exploit the tiny and unstable Direction-A headroom.

A10 Calibrated Residual Ensemble:
We further tested a supervised calibration strategy that learns a linear residual correction from multiple temporal candidates. The model is trained on calibration frames to approximate GT - FUGR using candidate residuals relative to FUGR. On the calibration split, a small correction scale gives only a tiny PSNR gain. However, on the held-out test split, every nonzero correction scale reduces PSNR and increases TDE. Even the test-oracle scale is 0.00, which is exactly FUGR-C. Therefore, the learned residual correction does not generalize, confirming that the remaining post-hoc refinement headroom is too small even for a calibrated ensemble.
